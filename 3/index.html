    Goal 3: Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages

    1. Home
    2. Goal 3
    Previous Goal
    Next Goal

    Targets

    Indicators

    3.1 By 2030, reduce the global maternal mortality ratio to less than 70 per 100,000 live births
    3.1.1   Reported online Maternal mortality ratio
     
    3.1.2   Reported online Proportion of births attended by skilled health personnel
     
    3.2 By 2030, end preventable deaths of newborns and children under 5 years of age, with all countries aiming to reduce neonatal mortality to at least as low as 12 per 1,000 live births and under‐5 mortality to at least as low as 25 per 1,000 live births
    3.2.1   Reported online Under-five mortality rate
     
    3.2.1.1   Reported online Infant mortality rate per 1000 born alive
    • National
     
    3.2.2   Reported online Neonatal mortality rate
     
    3.3 By 2030, end the epidemics of AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria and neglected tropical diseases and combat hepatitis, water-borne diseases and other communicable diseases
    3.3.1   Reported online Number of new HIV infections per 1,000 uninfected population, by sex, age and key populations
     
    3.3.1.1   Reported online HIV associated deaths per 100 000 population
    • National
     
    3.3.2   Reported online Tuberculosis incidence per 100,000 population
    • Global adjusted
     
    3.3.3   Exploring data sources Malaria incidence per 1,000 population
     
    3.3.4   Reported online Hepatitis B incidence per 100,000 population
     
    3.3.5   Exploring data sources Number of people requiring interventions against neglected tropical diseases
     
    3.4 By 2030, reduce by one third premature mortality from non-communicable diseases through prevention and treatment and promote mental health and well-being
    3.4.1   Reported online Mortality rate attributed to cardiovascular disease, cancer, diabetes or chronic respiratory disease
     
    3.4.2   Reported online Suicide mortality rate
     
    3.5 Strengthen the prevention and treatment of substance abuse, including narcotic drug abuse and harmful use of alcohol
    3.5.1   Reported online Coverage of treatment interventions (pharmacological, psychosocial and rehabilitation and aftercare services) for substance use disorders
     
    3.5.2   Reported online Harmful use of alcohol, defined according to the national context as alcohol per capita consumption (aged 15 years and older) within a calendar year in litres of pure alcohol
     
    3.5.2.1   Reported online Prevalence of harmful use of alcohol
    • National
     
    3.6 By 2020, halve the number of global deaths and injuries from road traffic accidents
    3.6.1   Reported online Death rate due to road traffic injuries
     
    3.7 By 2030, ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive health-care services, including for family planning, information and education, and the integration of reproductive health into national strategies and programmes
    3.7.1   Reported online Proportion of women of reproductive age (aged 15-49 years) who have their need for family planning satisfied with modern methods
     
    3.7.2   Reported online Adolescent birth rate (aged 15-19 years) per 1,000 women in that age group
     
    3.8 Achieve universal health coverage, including financial risk protection, access to quality essential health-care services and access to safe, effective, quality and affordable essential medicines and vaccines for all
    3.8.1   Reported online Coverage of essential health services (defined as the average coverage of essential services based on tracer interventions that include reproductive, maternal, newborn and child health, infectious diseases, non-communicable diseases and service capacity and access, among the general and the most disadvantaged population)
     
    3.8.1.1   Reported online Proportion of population having had access to essential medication
    • National
     
    3.8.2   Exploring data sources Proportion of population with large household expenditures on health as a share of total household expenditure or income
     
    3.8.2.1   Reported online Proportion of private expenditures (of households) for health
    • National
     
    3.8.2.2   Reported online Proportion of population with mandatory health insurance
    • National
     
    3.8.2.a   Reported online Proportion of population with household expenditures on health larger than 10% of total household expenditure
    • Global divided
     
    3.8.2.b   Reported online Proportion of population with household expenditures on health larger than 25% of total household expenditure
    • Global divided
     
    3.9 By 2030, substantially reduce the number of deaths and illnesses from hazardous chemicals and air, water and soil pollution and contamination
    3.9.1   Exploring data sources Mortality rate attributed to household and ambient air pollution
     
    3.9.2   Exploring data sources Mortality rate attributed to unsafe water, unsafe sanitation and lack of hygiene (exposure to unsafe Water, Sanitation and Hygiene for All (WASH) services)
     
    3.9.3   Reported online Mortality rate attributed to unintentional poisoning
     
    3.a Strengthen the implementation of the World Health Organization Framework Convention on Tobacco Control in all countries, as appropriate
    3.a.1   Reported online 3.a.1 Age-standardized prevalence of current tobacco use among persons aged 15 years and older
    • Global adjusted
     
    3.a.1.1   Reported online Proportion of 15 years old and over smoking population, by sex and age
    • National
     
    3.b Support the research and development of vaccines and medicines for the communicable and non‐communicable diseases that primarily affect developing countries, provide access to affordable essential medicines and vaccines, in accordance with the Doha Declaration on the TRIPS Agreement and Public Health, which affirms the right of developing countries to use to the full the provisions in the Agreement on Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights regarding flexibilities to protect public health, and, in particular, provide access to medicines for all
    3.b.1   Reported online Proportion of the target population covered by all vaccines included in their national programme
     
    3.b.2   Reported online Total net official development assistance to medical research and basic health sectors
    • Global adjusted
     
    3.b.3   Exploring data sources Proportion of health facilities that have a core set of relevant essential medicines available and affordable on a sustainable basis
     
    3.c Substantially increase health financing and the recruitment, development, training and retention of the health workforce in developing countries, especially in least developed countries and small island developing States
    3.c.1   Reported online Health worker density and distribution
    • Global adjusted
     
    3.d Strengthen the capacity of all countries, in particular developing countries, for early warning, risk reduction and management of national and global health risks
    3.d.1   Reported online International Health Regulations (IHR) capacity and health emergency preparedness
